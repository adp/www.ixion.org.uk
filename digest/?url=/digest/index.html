Re: Lectrickle query

Posted by "Clinton Gardiner" <clinton.gardiner-at-btinternet.com> on 27 April 2015 | 0 Comments

John Warr wrote:
> As some may know I have a touch of Raynaud’s syndrome in my paws
> which makes electric gloves a bit more than a luxury. I want to tidy
> up the fitting on the Harley which currently (pun) rubs from a fag
> lighter adapter. Harley don't do fuses. Like sensible folk they use
> thermal cut-outs so I went exploring under the side panel & found a
> vacant terminal which has 12v when the ignition is on. I have hung
> an eyelet off this to a socket for the glove controller and a direct
> earth for the negative terminal of the socket. I now have 12v
> switched at said socket. When I plug the controller into the socket
> however the power indicator light does not illuminate (which it did
> previously with the cig lighter doofus) and I appear
> to have about 2v between the terminal on the breaker and earth.
>
> Question
>
> Woss occurrin' dudes ?

There is a higher resistance between the battery and the breaker than the
heated grips have.

Heating elements tend to have a fairly low resistance. So it doesn't take a
lot of resistance to drop the voltage.

When you measure with no load the voltmeter has very high resistance so
there will be virtually no current flowing so the voltage will be high.

When you switch on the heaters they try to draw a lot of current. Voltage =
resistance times current means you are drawing enough current to drop 10
Volts across the resistance.

To find the high resistance you can either go through the wiring with a
resistance meter or measure the voltage at different points from the breaker
to the battery until it jumps to 12 volts.
